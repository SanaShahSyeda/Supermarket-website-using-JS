Root Vegetables

These are vegetables where the edible part is the underground portion of the plant. They store energy in the form of carbohydrates. Examples of root vegetables include carrots, beets, radishes, turnips, and sweet potatoes.all vegetables provide valuable nutrients.

Leafy Greens

This category includes vegetables with edible leaves. Leafy greens are rich in vitamins, minerals, and fiber. Examples include spinach, lettuce, kale, Swiss chard, and collard greens.all vegetables provide valuable nutrients and contribute to a healthy and balanced diet.

Stem & Shoot

These are vegetables where the edible part is the stem or shoot of the plant. They are typically tender and have a mild flavor. Examples include asparagus and celery.all vegetables provide valuable nutrients and contribute to a healthy and balanced diet.

Fruit Vegetables

This category includes vegetables that are botanically classified as fruits but are commonly used in savory dishes. These vegetables often have seeds and a soft flesh. Examples include tomatoes, cucumbers, eggplants (aubergines), and peppers.
